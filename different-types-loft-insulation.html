Types of Loft Insulation — Which Is Best?

A clear, local guide for Barking & Dagenham homeowners — we compare spray foam, mineral wool (Rockwool), fibreglass and loft rolls so you can choose the right option for your home and budget.

Quick overview

There are four common solutions used in UK lofts: mineral wool (Rockwool), fibreglass, quilted/roll insulation and spray foam. Each has trade-offs for cost, performance and installation. Below we explain the pros, cons and the types we recommend for Barking & Dagenham houses.

Mineral wool (Rockwool)

What it is: It's dense mineral fibres supplied as rolls or slabs and fitted between joists.

Why we recommend it: It gives excellent fire resistance, great acoustic properties and stable thermal performance it's ideal for older terraced and semi-detached properties common in Barking.

Typical cost range: mid-range; better long-term value.

Fibreglass

What it is: Glass-fibre batts or rolls laid on the loft floor.

Why homeowners choose it: cheapest professional option, easy to install and good thermal performance when laid correctly. Which is Good for quick, budget conscious upgrades.

Quilted / insulation rolls (yellow rolls)

What it is: Pre-formed rolls or quilted blankets laid between rafters or across the loft floor.

Why they’re common: widely available, easy for professionals and sometimes DIY. They’re effective when installed without gaps and at the right depth.

Spray foam

What it is: Liquid polyurethane sprayed into cavities where it cures into a sealed foam layer.

Pros: top tier R-value per mm and excellent air-seal which is great for awkward lofts or where airtightness is needed.

Cons: higher cost, specialist installers only, and can complicate future changes to the loft.

Which is best for Barking & Dagenham?

  • Budget & simple installs: fibreglass or quilted rolls.
  • Long-term safety & performance: mineral wool (Rockwool).
  • Best warm-roof / airtight solution: spray foam (professional only).
